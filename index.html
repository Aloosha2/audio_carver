Background

The seam carving algorithm was first introduced in 2007 for content-aware image resizing. The algorithm calculates "seams" in either the vertical or horizontal direction to be "carved out". Repeadetly carving these seams will reduce the image size while preserving content that is deemed important through the algorithm.

The motivation behind my work is to apply the seam carving algorithm to audio samples and remove content that is deemed uncessecary. This may be applied in both the vertical and horiozontal context in an audio sample, translating to time and frequency domains. Example audio samples are below.
